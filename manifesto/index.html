Killing the Vibe

Tech-optimists like Andrej Karpathy proclaim that “The hottest new programming language is English,” implying that everyone capable of reading this can save the world (or abandon it?) by vibe coding and join his “party in the Andromeda galaxy 1B years from now.” Designers currently use this technology to individually generate the same old tools for flashy filters on Instagram.

To produce anything out of the ordinary, one still has to learn to think like a programmer. This includes learning technical terms that describe what computers can do, understanding control structures like loops and conditionals, familiarizing oneself with open source, and breaking down problems.

To create the right thing in the right way, think like a designer:

  1. Don’t code. Not every problem needs a programmed solution. Nor does every designer need to code. The world was doing fine for long enough before it. Only critical reflection can ensure we are headed in the right direction.
  2. Don’t learn to code; code to learn. Capitalism pushes you to produce more, faster. Try to resist this. Ask questions, read, and value insight over output. LLMs are underrated as a learning tool. Demystifying technology allows steering its development.
  3. Don’t fully automate. Design systems seem like a good fit for coding, but nobody needs infinite random with machine-executed uniformity. Keep yourself involved. You are able to disrupt the system with a good idea.
  4. Look behind the façade. AI masks itself with emojis and imitates a friendly tone. It does not feel and it never will. Recognize corporate’s interest in tying users to their models. They won’t challenge your ideas because they want you to pay.
  5. Bring humans back in the loop. Big Tech promises complete autonomy with AI assistance. Collaborating with humans is more than just working together. People will lift your mood, surprise you, or challenge you with critical thought.
  6. Embrace embodiedness. Computer scientists try to distill the world into a problem. There is more to life than what can be expressed as text-based tokens. Build tools that bring digital design back to our physical, sensory reality.
  7. Don’t let the machine do the creative part. Do not ask for ideas, do not blindly accept its suggestions, and be cautious of “agents,” where AI goes on autopilot. Keep asking yourself: Is it necessary to code this? How does this benefit our planet?

People get so uncomfortable feeling their brain start to rattle that they stop it in its tracks and hand the task over to a chatbot. The infinitely submissive AI answers immediately, soothing the discomfort of uncertainty. Outsourcing thinking will have us aim for Andromeda while plummeting to the depths of a crumbling planet.
